Social

Dubai is a cosmpolitan city, and a melting pot of many different nationalities and cultures, but it's a lonely city. This project seeks to create a stage where inhabitants within a micro-community are encouraged to interact with one another, and foster a sense of community.

Site

Located in Jumeirah and within an ideal distance between public transporation methods, this site serves as a bridge between what is typically an exclusive high-end and somewhat elitist area, and other people from varying social stratas and cultural backgrounds.

Diagrams

Instead of placing a yard to either side of a single unit, it was placed right in the middle. This courtyard offers no physical seperation between neighbouring units.

Drawings

Ground Floor Plan

The courtyard sets the social stage here. I liken it to how mothers would force their children to play with the other kids back in kindergarten. Sure you can choose not to interact with your neighbours, but it's very inconvenient.

Side Note: That tilt? It allows the study to retain space while also providing a legally compliant car parking spot, and enough to space to exit from the car comfortably.

First Floor Plan

The first floor plan gives you an idea of how you can circumvent the circulation through "the bridge".

Imagine this scenario: You just came back from the supermarket. You have groceries you need to take to the kitchen.

Do you:

  1. Get your groceries and go to the kitchen, saying hello to your neighbours in the process?
  2. Avoid your neighbours at all cost, and carry the groceries up the stairs, across the brige, down the stairs, and into the kitchen?
