The 961

The 961 is the leading online English media site that caters to the Lebanese people in Lebanon, and abroad. The 961 features various engaging Lebanese content, news, media, and more.

The work with the 961 involved two projects: a re-branding for "the 961", and series of designs to be used on gift shop products for "the 961 foundation".

The Logo

When I was approached to design the logo, I was simply asked to modernise the existing logo. The goals were fairly vague, but they were made much clearer when it was established that my main goals were to:

  1. Create a logo consistent with the brand image
  2. Design a logo that evokes memories of Lebanon
  3. Create variations for different contexts
  4. Make a logo that is clean and clear

Brand Traits

The first step to be taken, before any design decision takes place, is to ask the question:

What is "The 961" about?

It's easy to design in a vacuum but a vacuum remains devoid of it's context. The following are the traits I found to be closely related to the brand after reviewing the content of it's website and what it offers.

Mood Board

Since nostalgia and evoking images of Lebanon are vital to the re-branding of the platform, a mood board was formed from vintage advertisement campaigns, posters, products, and signages that are associated with Lebanon.

Images used in the layout were pulled from Pinterest.

Typography and Colour

Raleway was selected as the main typeface due to the fact that the original logo was presented in Raleway. While this is a re-branding project, I believe that Raleway was a great choice, but more importantly I did not want to create a complete departure from the original logo.

By using the original typeface, I would be able to borrow from the pre-established brand identity, and still be able to create a fresher look and feel for the platform. I like to call this non-invasive re-branding - there would still be a sense of familiarity for returning users despite a new look being adopted.

Open Sans was an easy choice to make for body text since it's a typeface that compliments Raleway very well.

The colour palette was derived from the mood board and the brand traits. I arrived to a triad colour palette that'll allow the website to create a vibrant feel that evokes colours widely used in Lebanon's shops, and streets. The961 is a media website, but it isn't only a news agency, it's about anything, and everything Lebanon - Going with a triad made the most sense in this context.

Guiding Concept

If you've been to Lebanon then you've definitely had encounters with the ‘service’ in Lebanon and “the small but big” conversations taxi drivers tend to have.

The concept is to create a sense of Lebanese nostalgia of what is perhaps the most Lebanese experience in Lebanon. The Taxi ride serves as a hub of political, social, or comedic discourse.

The service spreads information, shares opinions, and creates discourse. When looked back on, the service is a common experience looked back on nostalgically, in the same manner we look back at Lebanon while living abroad.

Construction Process

The first step in the design process was to deconstruct the original logo into it's basics - minus the cedar tree.

The second step was to keep any text in lowercase format, and reduce the overall bulk of the logo. To reduce the bulk of the logo, I decided to use the "Light" version of the Raleway typeface on the first half of the brand name. Why? Simply put - 961 (Lebanon's country code) is far more important than "the".

Now it's time to add some colour. You'll notice that I switched to Arial in order to mimic the typefaces used on the street signs and car plates in Lebanon. The coloured field is then manipulated to start to mimic the appearance of the infamous 'service' (shared cab system in Lebanon) car plates.

Arial didn't quite make the cut, and at this point I decided I would go back to Raleway.

The 961 Shop

Designs for a NGO

The 961 Foundation is associated with "The 961". The main scope of the work was to prepare graphic design elements that could be used in a variety of gift shop items in order to raise funds through the foundation's "The 961 Shop". All proceeds from the purchases will go to help the Lebanese people.

Almost all of the Arabic-script designs feature a custom script that I had designed specifically for this project. These can be distinguished through their highly orthogonal and rectilinear forms.
