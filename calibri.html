Calibri

Calibri is a pub that is situated in Beirut in the bustling streets of Hamra. The pub was taken on for a comprehensive re-branding project to create a consistent design language reflective of it's vision, patrons, and reputation.

Branding

Logo and Brandmark

The temptation was too big to avoid the word play between Calibri the pub, and Calibri the font. This bit of playfulness managed to amplify that bit of a cheeky spirit both Calibris have. It also makes it easier for me to spot other designers in the pub.

Below you can find an assortment of accepted brand colours. Admittedly, they're a bit daring - But that's exactly what Calibri is about. The reasoning behind these colour choices was to ensure that any poster, or coaster, or any other non-architectural design element would contrast and stand out from the interior of the space.

The tag-line “You’ll never drink alone” pays an homage to the fact that Calibri is the meeting spot of (ugh…) the Liverpool fan club in Lebanon.

Menu Design

The menu required a different kind of research. Calibri’s menu needed to be re-studied since there was a lack of variety of profitable finger foods. The research can be broken down into 3 steps:

  1. Find out what foods are profitable and generate the most sales.
  2. Encourage customers to purchase based on their cravings, and not the cost.
  3. Create a menu that has variety but is short enough as to not overwhelm and confuse the customers.

Architecture

Plans and Sections

The changes proposed would relocate the bar to better make use of the column sitting in the middle of the room so that it becomes a feature rather than just a poorly placed column in the room.

The pub originally seated 58 patrons. After optimising seating patterns and distributions, the pub can now allow up to 69 patrons, or a 19% increase in potential seated customers.

Since Calibri is being described as a warm speak-easy pub, and is defined by a tight knit community of regulars, the pre-dominant materials chosen for the interior of the pub were clay bricks, and oakwood.

The Music Tree

The central column takes on a far more important role than simply being an inconvenience. The columns now functions as the main, and only source of music. I like to call it The Music Tree. The Music Tree has 3 large amplifiers that have been carefully placed in order to smoothly distribute the sound coming from the amplifiers around the pub.

The Foldtable

The FoldTable came out of a necessity to create space on the stage for band nights, and the lack of a store room where tables can be stowed away. The best part? You can easily a hang a poster on the underside of the table, which frames the poster when folded up.

Bar Details

Two important details that ought to be highlighted:

  1. The typical bar section, along with the hidden LED light strip to illuminate the floor. How many times have you dropped something and couldn’t find it because it was way too dark to see?
  2. The store-front bar allows the pub to capitalise on potential customers looking for a quick drink but aren't necessarily willing to commit to sitting inside, and to allow patrons to stand outside and still be visually connected to the pub.

Storefront Design

The current signage isn't that visible while walking down the street. The idea is to bring the signage down to your eye-level, right in your face.
